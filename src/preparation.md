## Constructing Solutions ğŸ“’ ğŸ–Šï¸
 
Writing code is mostly the act of putting an idea onto paper. These ideas are ones in which we need to process and visualise.

The **Notebook** is a powerful tool here, to illustrate this point, it can be used for:

* Using squares and lines to represent the relationship between objects in code.
* Writing out the steps of an algorithm you are designing and then implementing it.
* Showing the relationship between function calls.
* Creating notes and a summary of the task at hand and what to focus on.
* A deconstruction of the problem into smaller pieces.

The above examples are skills you will need to develop to improve as a programmer and problem solver.

For your own self, it also helps to ease the cognitive load when you are constructing a solution. Any complex problem is going to require a lot of notes and information being accessible and there is going to be a limit on what you can hold in your head and memorise.

## Knowing what you have done ğŸ“’

In combination with the notebook, A journal helps keep track of what solutions you have tried, what you still are attempting to resolve and gives you a points of reflection.

Software projects can require us to take on different tasks at different points in time and remembering where we were at can be difficult.

Journaling what we have done and where we left off, gives us a way to return back to where we left something, what has been completed and the reasons why. It also provides accountability.

When a study or coding session concludes, make sure you make notes on what needs to be worked on and what you need to do to continue.

## Always Remember!

Each time you go into a study session, make sure you have all the equipment and do a quick preflight check-list.

* Laptop is open and windows are tiled with a code editor and documentation open
* Notebook is open to the last page you used with a blank page available
* Pen is ready for drawing up solutions and noting ideas
* Catch up on any information or notes you left for yourself from last session
* Resume from where you left off

This can help with optimising the time you have with your studying and coding sessions.
